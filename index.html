On Our Own Terms

Explore the top words used in American left & right online news headlines each week.

The US online news system has been increasingly defined by polarization, more specifically a phenomenon described as "asymmetric polarization". Since 2016, media researchers have found evidence of this over and over again; conservative media sources increasingly are dominated by extreme voices, while liberal sources are available across the spectrum of politics. In many ways the two set of media are talking about completely differing things.

To understand that, this site lets you explore the top words used in article headlines across sets of American online news sources. These partisan sets are based on sharing patterns of Twitter users, and thus more of an assessment of what registered voters from each party are sharing and thus an indirect assessment of the political position of the publication itself. This data is collected from an evolving new archive of online news being collaboratively built by the Media Cloud organization and the Internet Archive's Wayback Machine.
